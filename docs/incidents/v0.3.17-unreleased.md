## What happened

The Signed Release Ceremony (SRT) failed **fail-closed** on the Owner PC, before signing or
publishing anything. The producer compared the wheelhouse identity recorded by Phase B on the RPi2
against the value it recomputed on Windows, and they disagreed.

## Root cause

The wheelhouse identity was `sha256(pack_tree(members))`. `pack_tree()` produces a **gzip** archive,
so the identity was a digest over *compressed* bytes.

Cross-runtime evidence isolated it precisely:

| | RPi2 — CPython 3.10.12 / zlib 1.2.11 | Windows — CPython 3.14.4 / zlib-ng 1.3.1 |
|---|---|---|
| raw uncompressed tar | `fea3b15b…f56280`, 13,486,080 B | `fea3b15b…f56280`, 13,486,080 B |
| gzip output | `47f1449a…5c7cd0`, 13,298,416 B | `3852c706…49e2c4`, 13,299,626 B |

The **raw tar was byte-identical**, which ruled out wheel mutation, path-set drift, SHA256SUMS
mutation, transfer corruption, and tar canonicalisation drift. Both gzip headers began
`1f8b08000000000002ff` (mtime zeroed). The divergence was entirely the DEFLATE stream — an
implementation detail of whichever zlib the runtime links.

`pack_tree()`'s canonicalisation (sorted members, `mtime=0`, mode `0644`, uid/gid `0`) all lives in
the tar layer; nothing constrained the compressor. Its docstring claimed *"Two runs over identical
content yield identical bytes"* — true within one runtime, silently false across two, which is
exactly what a build-on-Pi / sign-on-PC workflow requires.

## Why it was not caught earlier

`test_pack_tree_is_deterministic` called `pack_tree` **twice in one process**, varying only dict
insertion order. It proved order-independence, never cross-runtime byte stability, and there was no
golden vector. It passed throughout and gave false confidence.

## What was NOT wrong

The Phase-B bundle itself was sound: exactly 30 wheels (6 source-built + 24 authorized reused),
SHA256SUMS validated, runtime-lock bijection, image-context binding, reuse authorization, plus a
successful offline install and runtime validation on the real RPi2. Only the *identity function*
was defective.

## Correction (v0.3.18 — implemented in code, not yet released)

Wheelhouse identity moved to the **Logical Tree Digest v1** (`release/logical_tree.py`) — a
domain-separated, length-prefixed SHA-256 over the exact 31-member mapping, with no compression and
no `tarfile`. Manifest **format 3** carries exactly one wheelhouse identity; the gzip-derived
`bundle_sha256` was removed with no compatibility mode. `pack_tree()` survives **only** to produce
final artifact bytes, whose SHA-256 is taken over the exact published bytes and signed.

Raw tar was considered as the identity and rejected: `tarfile` emits a PAX extended header for
paths over 100 bytes (the 113-byte websockets wheel triggers this), and `DEFAULT_FORMAT` itself
changed in CPython 3.8 — so raw tar narrows the drift class without removing it.

## Disposition

The `v0.3.17` tag and all failure evidence in the continuation ledger remain **untouched**. The old
provenance was **not** migrated or regenerated: v0.3.18 reruns Phase B from its own commit and
produces fresh artifacts. Retagging was rejected — a public tag must keep meaning one object,
because `source.tag` is bound into the signed manifest.
